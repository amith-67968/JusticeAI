INFORMATION TECHNOLOGY ACT, 2000 — CYBER CRIME PROVISIONS
============================================================

The Information Technology Act, 2000 (IT Act) is India's primary legislation governing cyber crimes, electronic commerce, and digital signatures. It was amended significantly in 2008.

KEY SECTIONS
------------

Section 43 — Damage to Computer, Computer System, etc.:
If any person without permission accesses, downloads, introduces a virus, disrupts, denies access, or damages any computer system, they shall be liable to pay damages by way of compensation up to Rs. 5 crores to the affected person.

Section 65 — Tampering with Computer Source Documents:
Whoever knowingly or intentionally conceals, destroys, or alters any computer source code used for a computer, computer programme, or computer system shall be punished with imprisonment up to 3 years, or with fine up to Rs. 2 lakhs, or with both.

Section 66 — Computer Related Offences (Hacking):
If any person dishonestly or fraudulently does any act referred to in Section 43, they shall be punished with imprisonment up to 3 years or with fine up to Rs. 5 lakhs or with both. This is the main "hacking" provision.

Section 66A — Sending Offensive Messages (STRUCK DOWN):
This section was struck down by the Supreme Court of India in Shreya Singhal v. Union of India (2015) as unconstitutional for violating Article 19(1)(a) — freedom of speech and expression.

Section 66B — Punishment for Dishonestly Receiving Stolen Computer Resource:
Imprisonment up to 3 years, or fine up to Rs. 1 lakh, or both.

Section 66C — Punishment for Identity Theft:
Whoever fraudulently or dishonestly makes use of the electronic signature, password or any other unique identification feature of any other person shall be punished with imprisonment up to 3 years and fine up to Rs. 1 lakh.

Section 66D — Punishment for Cheating by Personation Using Computer Resource:
Whoever by means of any communication device or computer resource cheats by personation shall be punished with imprisonment up to 3 years and fine up to Rs. 1 lakh.

Section 66E — Punishment for Violation of Privacy:
Whoever intentionally captures, publishes, or transmits the image of a private area of any person without their consent shall be punished with imprisonment up to 3 years or fine up to Rs. 2 lakhs or both.

Section 67 — Publishing or Transmitting Obscene Material:
First conviction: imprisonment up to 3 years and fine up to Rs. 5 lakhs.
Subsequent conviction: imprisonment up to 5 years and fine up to Rs. 10 lakhs.

Section 67A — Publishing Sexually Explicit Material:
First conviction: imprisonment up to 5 years and fine up to Rs. 10 lakhs.
Subsequent conviction: imprisonment up to 7 years and fine up to Rs. 10 lakhs.

Section 67B — Child Pornography:
First conviction: imprisonment up to 5 years and fine up to Rs. 10 lakhs.
Subsequent conviction: imprisonment up to 7 years and fine up to Rs. 10 lakhs.

COMMON CYBER CRIMES AND APPLICABLE SECTIONS
--------------------------------------------

1. Online Banking Fraud / UPI Fraud → Sections 43, 66, 66C, 66D
2. Phishing → Section 66D (Cheating by Personation)
3. Hacking → Sections 43, 66
4. Identity Theft → Section 66C
5. Cyber Stalking → Section 354D IPC (Section 78 BNS)
6. Online Defamation → Section 499/500 IPC (Section 356 BNS)
7. Email Spoofing → Sections 66C, 66D
8. Data Breach → Sections 43, 43A, 72A
9. Ransomware → Sections 43, 66, 66A (if applicable)
10. Social Media Abuse → Sections 66C, 66D, 67

CYBER CRIME REPORTING
---------------------

1. National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal: https://cybercrime.gov.in
   - File complaints for financial fraud, women/child-related cyber crimes, and other cyber crimes
   - Track complaint status online

2. Cyber Crime Helpline: 1930 (toll-free, 24x7)
   - Immediate assistance for online financial fraud
   - Helps in freezing suspected fraudulent transactions

3. Local Police Station: File an FIR under IT Act sections at any police station

4. Cyber Crime Cells: Most states have dedicated cyber crime police stations / cells

IMPORTANT: Report financial cyber fraud within the "Golden Hour" (first few hours) by calling 1930 to maximise chances of recovering funds.

DIGITAL PERSONAL DATA PROTECTION ACT, 2023
--------------------------------------------
India's data protection law gives individuals rights over their personal data:
- Right to access and correct data
- Right to erase data
- Right to grievance redressal
- Data fiduciaries must obtain consent and implement security safeguards
- Significant penalties for data breaches (up to Rs. 250 crores)
